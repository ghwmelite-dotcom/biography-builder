The Life of Grace

◆

Grace Amara Johnson was born on March 10, 1950, in Accra, Ghana, to the late Samuel and Elizabeth Mensah. The third of five children, Grace grew up surrounded by love, laughter, and the rich cultural traditions of her homeland. From a young age, she displayed an extraordinary warmth that drew people to her, a quality that would define her entire life.

Grace attended Achimota School, where she excelled in her studies and developed a deep passion for teaching. She earned her Bachelor of Education from the University of Ghana in 1972, and soon after began what would become a 35-year career in education. She touched the lives of thousands of students, many of whom remember her as the teacher who believed in them when no one else did.

In 1975, Grace married the love of her life, Robert Kwame Johnson, in a beautiful ceremony at the Accra Ridge Church. Together they built a home filled with faith, music, and an open door for anyone in need. Their union was blessed with four children: Samuel, Elizabeth, Kwame Jr., and Abena.

Grace was a pillar of her community and a devoted member of Grace Community Chapel, where she served as the choir director for over two decades. Her powerful soprano voice was a gift she shared generously, and Sunday mornings were never quite the same without her leading the congregation in praise.

After retiring from teaching in 2007, Grace dedicated herself to mentoring young women in her community, establishing the Amara Foundation, which has provided scholarships to over 200 girls across West Africa. She believed that education was the key to unlocking every young woman's potential.

Grace Amara Johnson passed peacefully on November 20, 2025, surrounded by her loving family. She is survived by her devoted husband Robert, her four children, twelve grandchildren, and a legacy of love that will endure for generations.
