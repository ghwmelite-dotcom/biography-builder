## 4. FAQ Schema Content — 20 Questions & Answers

**Q1: How much does a funeral cost in Ghana?**
A: The cost of a funeral in Ghana ranges from GHS 15,000 for a simple burial to GHS 200,000 or more for an elaborate ceremony. The main expenses include mortuary fees, casket, venue rental, catering, brochure printing, and transportation.

**Q2: What is included in a funeral brochure?**
A: A standard Ghanaian funeral brochure includes a front cover with the deceased's photo and dates, a biography, tributes from family members, the order of service, hymn lyrics, acknowledgements, and a back cover. Most brochures are 8 to 16 pages long.

**Q3: How do I design a funeral brochure online?**
A: You can design a funeral brochure online using FuneralPress. Choose a template, add your photos and text, select hymns from our library, and download a print-ready PDF. The entire process takes 30 minutes to an hour.

**Q4: What is a one-week celebration in Ghana?**
A: A one-week celebration (also called "one week observation" or "adeto") is held one week after a person's death. Family and friends gather to mourn, share memories, and discuss funeral arrangements. It is a deeply rooted Akan tradition observed across Ghana.

**Q5: How do I write a tribute for a funeral?**
A: Start by noting your relationship to the deceased. Share specific memories and qualities you admired. Keep the tone respectful and personal. A good tribute is 300 to 500 words long.

**Q6: What hymns are commonly sung at Ghanaian funerals?**
A: Popular funeral hymns in Ghana include "Abide with Me," "The Lord Is My Shepherd," "Rock of Ages," and Twi hymns like "Awurade Kasa." FuneralPress has a library of over 11,000 hymns.

**Q7: Can I plan a funeral in Ghana from abroad?**
A: Yes. Many Ghanaian diaspora families plan funerals remotely. You can delegate on-the-ground tasks to a trusted family member while handling brochure design, memorial pages, and invitations online.

**Q8: What is the order of service at a Ghanaian funeral?**
A: A typical order includes: opening prayer, hymn, biography reading, tributes, sermon, hymn, filing past the body, closing prayer, and then the burial. A reception usually follows.

**Q9: How do I create an online memorial page?**
A: On FuneralPress, you can create a free online memorial page by entering the deceased's details, uploading photos, and sharing the unique link with family and friends.

**Q10: What is an Aseda cloth label?**
A: An Aseda cloth label is a small printed label attached to the funeral cloth given to mourners as a keepsake. It typically includes the deceased's photo, name, dates, and a message of thanks.

**Q11: How do I write a death announcement in Ghana?**
A: Include the full name and title of the deceased, date and place of death, the surviving family, funeral date and venue, and any dress code instructions.

**Q12: What should I wear to a funeral in Ghana?**
A: Traditional Ghanaian funerals use red and black cloth for close family. Some families designate specific cloth colours. White is often worn for elderly deceased persons as a celebration of life.

**Q13: How far in advance should I plan a funeral in Ghana?**
A: Most Ghanaian funerals are planned 4 to 12 weeks after death, as the body is preserved at the mortuary during this period.

**Q14: What is the 40-day celebration after death?**
A: The 40-day observation is practised in some Ghanaian communities, particularly among Catholics. It involves prayers, a church service, and a family gathering.

**Q15: How do I create a funeral guest book online?**
A: FuneralPress allows you to create a digital guest book that you share via a link or QR code. Attendees can sign the book and leave messages from their phones.

**Q16: Can I add QR codes to funeral brochures?**
A: Yes. FuneralPress can generate a QR code that links to the deceased's online memorial page, guest book, or photo gallery.

**Q17: What is the role of the family head in a Ghanaian funeral?**
A: The family head (abusuapanyin) officially announces the death, leads family meetings, approves the funeral date, oversees customary rites, and receives donations.

**Q18: How do I track funeral donations and expenses?**
A: Use FuneralPress's Budget Planner to record all income and expenses. This creates transparency and ensures funds are properly accounted for.

**Q19: What Bible verses are appropriate for a funeral brochure cover?**
A: Popular verses include Psalm 23:1, John 11:25, Revelation 14:13, and Psalm 116:15.

**Q20: How many copies of the funeral brochure should I print?**
A: Expected guests plus 20%. For 300 guests, print 350 to 400 copies. Alternatively, share a digital version to reduce costs.
